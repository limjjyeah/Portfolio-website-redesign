Brush Strokes — paint on paper

paint directly on paper. no planning, no corrections. whatever wants to come through, comes through. each mark an attempt to catch something that can't quite be named.

there's a directness to painting that collage doesn't have — no found image to hide behind, no existing texture to borrow. it's just the gesture. the speed of the hand. working in this way, i notice what i'm avoiding and what i'm drawn toward before my mind has a chance to intervene.
